# Sacred Political Order Definition

> *Formerly titled "Symbolic Order Definition." The term was updated to "sacred political order" to foreground the Durkheimian sacred-object relationship that generates the workbench's core research question. See core-definitions.md for the full rationale.*

A sacred political order is a durable structure of shared meaning organized around sacred objects, values, or persons — things whose violation is collectively experienced as desecration, not merely harm. It defines collective identity, legitimates authority, organizes memory and moral judgment, and specifies the goods for which members may be asked to sacrifice.

## Distinctions

| Term | Scope | Unit of analysis |
|---|---|---|
| Symbolic field | Broad background of human meaning | Usually no |
| Sacred political order | Durable structure organized around sacred objects, values, or persons; defines collective identity, legitimacy, sacrifice | Yes |
| Embodied institutional order | Material and organizational form that lets a sacred political order act | Yes |

## Sacred Political Order Strength

Sacred Political Order Strength measures the degree to which a sacred political order coherently organizes collective identity, legitimacy, obligation, memory, moral judgment, and sacrifice around its sacred objects, values, or persons.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | No coherent collective sacred political order is identifiable. |
| 1 | Weak or temporary order; sacred objects have limited authority. |
| 2 | Shared identity exists but the sacred objects have limited authority over persons. |
| 3 | Durable order organizes identity, legitimacy, memory, and obligation but remains limited and corrigible. |
| 4 | Strong order organizes belonging, duty, memory, and moral judgment around its sacred objects, with meaningful sacrifice expected. |
| 5 | Totalizing order; the sacred objects define ultimate loyalty, sacred meaning, enemyhood, and terms of suffering, killing, dying, or conscience surrender. |

Scores of 4 or 5 require evidence of obligation or sacrifice, not merely visible symbols.
