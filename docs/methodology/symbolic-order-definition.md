# Symbolic Order Definition

A symbolic order is a durable structure of shared meaning that defines collective identity, legitimates authority, organizes memory and moral judgment, and specifies the goods for which members may be asked to sacrifice.

## Distinctions

| Term | Scope | Unit of analysis |
|---|---|---|
| Symbolic field | Broad background of human meaning | Usually no |
| Symbolic order | Durable structure of shared identity, legitimacy, memory, judgment, and sacrifice | Yes |
| Embodied institutional order | Material and organizational form that lets a symbolic order act | Yes |

## Symbolic Order Strength

Symbolic Order Strength measures the degree to which a symbolic order coherently organizes collective identity, legitimacy, obligation, memory, moral judgment, and sacrifice.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 0 | No coherent collective symbolic order is identifiable. |
| 1 | Weak or temporary symbolic order. |
| 2 | Shared identity exists but has limited authority over persons. |
| 3 | Durable symbolic order organizes identity, legitimacy, memory, and obligation but remains limited and corrigible. |
| 4 | Strong symbolic order organizes belonging, duty, memory, and moral judgment, with meaningful sacrifice expected. |
| 5 | Totalizing symbolic order defines ultimate loyalty, sacred meaning, enemyhood, and terms of suffering, killing, dying, or conscience surrender. |

Scores of 4 or 5 require evidence of obligation or sacrifice, not merely visible symbols.
