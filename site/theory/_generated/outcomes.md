The theory distinguishes six outcome types based on how an embodied sacred political order responds when its self-preservation drive encounters crisis pressure.

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| **Sacrificial Escalation** | The collective becomes sacred, enemies become existential, and suffering, killing, or dying become morally required. |
| **Restrained Reordering** | The order reforms through pluralism, dissent, constitutional limits, anti-sacrificial memory, and distributed authority. |
| **Collapse** | The order loses legitimacy, coherence, or command capacity. |
| **Absorption / Transformation** | The old order survives partially by being incorporated into a successor order. |
| **Stagnation / Frozen Pathology** | The system persists through repression, ritualized ideology, inertia, or fear. |
| **Hybrid / Transitional** | Multiple outcomes occur in sequence or tension. |

## Sacrificial Escalation

The collective becomes sacred, enemies become existential, and suffering, killing, or dying become morally required.

*Gold cases:* **Imperial Japan**, **Nazi Germany**

*Comparators:* Khmer Rouge Cambodia, Maoist China, Revolutionary France, World War I Nationalisms

---

## Restrained Reordering

The order reforms through pluralism, dissent, constitutional limits, anti-sacrificial memory, and distributed authority.

*Gold cases:* **Postwar Germany**, **United States after Vietnam**

*Comparators:* Canada, European Union, Gandhian Indian Independence, Switzerland

---

## Collapse

The order loses legitimacy, coherence, or command capacity.

*Gold cases:* **Soviet Union Collapse**

*Comparators:* Confederacy, Western Roman Empire

---

## Absorption / Transformation

The old order survives partially by being incorporated into a successor order.

*Comparators:* British Empire to Commonwealth

---

## Stagnation / Frozen Pathology

The system persists through repression, ritualized ideology, inertia, or fear.

*Comparators:* North Korea

---

## Hybrid / Transitional

Multiple outcomes occur in sequence or tension.

*Comparators:* American Civil War / Union, Post-9/11 United States, Soviet Communism
